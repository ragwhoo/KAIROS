Semantic Colors — The Signals

Designed as a unified temperature system. Blue (cool, focus) → Green (balanced, complete) → Amber (warm, caution) → Red (hot, urgent). They form a natural spectrum, not a random color pick.

How they relate: These sit at ~60° (blue), ~140° (green), ~40° (amber), ~0° (red) on the hue wheel — a logical clockwise sweep from cool to warm. No clashing, no random picks.

Why This Works

Generic Palette

Random hex values pulled from different systems. Blue here, orange there. Text gray is too light to read. No hierarchy, no relationship.

Kairos Palette

All neutrals are cool-leaning with consistent undertone. Accent has full shade range for states. Semantic colors form a temperature spectrum. Text is pure black at top with clear gray steps.
